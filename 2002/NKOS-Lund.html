NKOS Special Meeting at the NetLab conference, April 12, 2002

Friday 12th of April, 14.30-17.30

NKOS Special Meeting: Discussion of standard developments regarding the use of thesauri, classification and other KO systems on the net

Participation, Goal

This is intended to be a discussion meeting between a few experts and experienced colleagues in this field of work. There will probably be short statements but no formal presentations.

All interested participants at the NetLab conference are invited to join this meeting, however, provided they accept this formula of the meeting and under the condition that there is enough space in the meeting room.

The goal of the meeting is to start a discussion between people involved in work with different encoding and modelling approaches for tesauri and classification systems and similar.
We intend to make sure that we make an effort to prevent a situation where the concepts and understandings of what a tesaurus or classification system is and how it should be used do differ widely.

Topics and references

Hopefully the speakers from the knowledge organization/semantic web session can do informal presentations at this special meeting as well (cf. the presentations linked to the conference programme page), more specific to the encoding and standardization approaches, problems, examples:
  • Joseph Busch about the XML DTD approach and the Voc-ML project (XML Schema. Strawman DTD)
  • Phil Cross about RDF Schema approaches (RDF Schema for Thesauri)
  • Diane Vizine-Goetz about the OCLC plans for a taxonomy service and encoding issues related to that

  • Possibly Sebastian Hammer (Indexdata) can contribute with his experiences from the ZThes (Zthes: a Z39.50 Profile for Thesaurus Navigation Version 0.4) effort and implementations.

Linda Hill pointed me to the ADL Thesaurus Protocol work carried out in the Alexandria Digital Earth Prototype (ADEPT) project (specification.html) which represents yet another approach.

Another issue should be to discuss, if and how we could participate in the work of the EU Network of Excellence OntoWeb and their upcoming activities.

In addition we should have a similar discussion related to the W3C Semantic Web activities and their Web Ontology Working Group.
Please consult the "Requirements for a Web Ontology Language" document at: www.w3.org/TR/webont-req/ (Maybe Dan Brickley could make contributions here).

We might discuss if and how to continue to raise these NKOS issues at conferences like the Sixth European Conference on Research and Advanced Technology for Digital Libraries , ECDL 2002, Sept 16-18, 2002, Rome, Italy and others.

Please feel free to invite other interested people to this meeting and to share thoughts and references with the rest of us.
